1. Introduction

Klu Health, Inc. ("Klu," "we," "us," or "our") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our mobile application, website, and other services (collectively, the "Services").

Please read this Privacy Policy carefully. By using our Services, you consent to the collection, use, and sharing of your information as described in this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree with our policies and practices, do not use our Services.

2. Information We Collect

We collect several types of information from and about users of our Services:

2.1 Information You Provide to Us

When you use our Services, we may collect the following types of information:

  • Account Information: When you create an account, we collect your email address, password, age, and other profile information.
  • Health and Biometric Information: We collect information about your menstrual cycle, symptoms, mood, sexual activity, contraception use, and other health-related information you choose to provide.
  • Survey Responses: Information you provide when responding to surveys, questionnaires, or assessments within our Services.
  • Communications: Information you provide when you contact us for customer support or otherwise communicate with us.
  • Payment Information: If you purchase a premium subscription, we may collect payment information, though this is typically processed by our third-party payment processors.

2.2 Information We Collect Automatically

When you use our Services, we may automatically collect certain information, including:

  • Device Information: Information about your device, including device type, operating system, unique device identifiers, IP address, mobile network information, and device settings.
  • Usage Information: Information about your interaction with our Services, such as features you use, time spent on the Services, pages or screens viewed, and navigation paths.
  • Log Data: Server logs, which may include information like IP address, browser type, pages visited, time spent on pages, access times, and referring website addresses.
  • Location Information: General location information (such as country or city) derived from your IP address.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for various purposes, including to:

  • Provide and maintain our Services, including tracking menstrual cycles and providing health insights.
  • Personalize your experience with our Services and deliver content relevant to your interests.
  • Improve and develop our Services, including through data analysis, research, and testing.
  • Communicate with you, including sending notifications, responding to inquiries, and providing customer support.
  • Protect the safety, integrity, and security of our Services, users, and others.
  • Enforce our Terms of Service and other policies.
  • Comply with legal obligations and respond to legal requests.

3.1 Processing of Health Information

As a health tracking app, we process sensitive health information to provide our core Services. We use this information to:

  • Track your menstrual cycle and predict future cycles.
  • Provide insights into your reproductive health and wellbeing.
  • Personalize content, recommendations, and insights based on your health data.
  • Deliver personalized health information based on your cycle phase and health profile.

3.2 De-identified and Aggregated Information

We may de-identify and aggregate information collected through our Services and use it for research, analytics, and improving our Services. This information does not identify you personally and may be shared with third parties for research or business purposes.

4. How We Share Information

We may share your information in the following circumstances:

4.1 With Your Consent

We may share your information when you explicitly consent to the sharing.

4.2 Service Providers

We share information with third-party vendors, service providers, contractors, or agents who perform services for us or on our behalf. These third parties are contractually obligated to protect your information and are limited in how they may use your information.

4.3 Business Transfers

If we are involved in a merger, acquisition, sale of assets, bankruptcy, reorganization, dissolution, or similar transaction or proceeding, your information may be transferred as part of that transaction.

4.4 Compliance with Laws and Protection

We may disclose your information to comply with applicable laws and regulations, to respond to a subpoena, court order, or other legal request, or when we believe in good faith that disclosure is necessary to protect our rights, protect your safety or the safety of others, investigate fraud, or respond to a government request.

4.5 De-identified and Aggregated Data

We may share de-identified and aggregated information with third parties for research, analytics, and business purposes. This information cannot reasonably be used to identify you.

5. Your Rights and Choices

You have several rights and choices regarding your information:

5.1 Account Information

You can review and update your account information through your account settings within our Services.

5.2 Communication Preferences

You can opt out of receiving promotional emails from us by following the unsubscribe instructions in those emails. You may not opt out of service-related communications (e.g., account verification, transactional communications, changes/updates to features of the Services, technical and security notices).

5.3 Privacy Settings

Our Services may include settings that allow you to control your privacy preferences, such as choosing what information is visible to other users or whether to use certain features.

5.4 Data Access and Portability

Depending on your location, you may have the right to request access to your personal information, receive a copy of your data in a structured, machine-readable format, or request that we transmit this information to another service provider (where technically feasible).

5.5 Data Deletion

You can request deletion of your account and personal information. We will delete or de-identify your information, unless we need to retain certain information for legitimate business or legal purposes.

5.6 Objection and Restriction

In certain jurisdictions, you may have the right to object to or request restriction of processing of your personal information.

To exercise these rights, please contact us using the contact information provided at the end of this Privacy Policy.

6. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect the security of your personal information. However, no method of transmission over the Internet or electronic storage is 100% secure. Therefore, while we strive to use commercially acceptable means to protect your personal information, we cannot guarantee its absolute security.

We maintain security measures to protect your information from unauthorized access, alteration, or destruction, including:

  • Encryption of sensitive data in transit and at rest
  • Regular security assessments and testing
  • Access controls and authentication procedures
  • Regular monitoring for unauthorized access

7. Children's Privacy

Our Services are not directed to children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you are a parent or guardian and you believe your child has provided us with personal information, please contact us. If we become aware that we have collected personal information from a child under age 13 without verification of parental consent, we will take steps to remove that information from our servers.

8. International Data Transfers

Your information may be transferred to, stored, and processed in countries other than the country in which you reside. These countries may have data protection laws that are different from those in your country. By using our Services, you consent to the transfer of your information to countries outside your country of residence, including the United States.

We take steps to ensure that your information receives an adequate level of protection, regardless of where it is processed.

9. Philippines Privacy Rights

If you are a Philippines resident, the Data Privacy Act of 2012 provides you with specific rights regarding your personal information. This section describes your privacy rights under Philippines law and explains how to exercise those rights.

9.1 Right to Know

You have the right to request that we disclose certain information to you about our collection, use, disclosure, and processing of your personal information.

9.2 Right to Delete

You have the right to request that we delete any of your personal information that we collected from you and retained, subject to certain exceptions.

9.3 Right to Non-Discrimination

We will not discriminate against you for exercising any of your privacy rights.

9.4 Exercising Your Rights

To exercise your rights described above, please submit a verifiable request to us by contacting us using the information provided at the end of this Privacy Policy.

10. Updates to this Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or for other operational, legal, or regulatory reasons. We will notify you of any material changes by posting the updated Privacy Policy on this page and updating the "Last Updated" date at the top of this Privacy Policy.

We encourage you to review this Privacy Policy periodically to stay informed about our information practices and your choices.
